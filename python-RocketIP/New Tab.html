Frequently Asked Questions

Why would I pay for book suggestions? Are you mad?!

The books you see on here aren't picked to suit an agenda: there are no commercial, social, political, or even educational goals of any kind. The only agenda here is pure intellectual diversity.

That means there's nothing else in the world quite like it.

While most other book-related services are free, they're usually funded by (and therefore controlled by) book sales, marketers, venture capital, private donors, etc. As an independent venture, 100 Million Books is bound by none of those forces. It's driven by uninhibited freedom. If you value this singular mission, consider Gold!

But I'm already going broke buying books! Why don't you just take commissions from those sales?

An organization is controlled by those who pay it. Taking commissions would motivate us to add books based on sales instead of intrigue. That would make us no different from the numerous booksellers already out there, and you probably wouldn't like that very much.

Ditto for ads. Paid book placements and other marketing-driven content would dilute the mistique of our collection.

Okay, but why a subscription? It's just a silly extension...

Adding books to the collection takes an enormous amount of time. Most snippets on the service are original discoveries—i.e., they're not available anywhere else except the original publication. There's no API these snippets are being 'pulled' from.

Each snippet is thoroughly verified & vetted before it's added. Specifically, the process goes like this: the original publication is found, a snippet is located, the content is verified, and formatting is added to ensure what you see on the service is 100% authentic. It's a grueling process that takes hours and hours every month. Revenue from subscriptions funds this work, as well as ongoing technical development.

Also, keep in mind that browser extensions are merely the best vehicles to deliver the service right now. In a few years, that might change. But the data itself—this ever-growing store of ideas that embodies the wisdom of those who came before us so it isn't lost by those who come after us—will remain relevant forever. So you're not just paying for an extension—you're helping build something that will last for generations. That's a big deal!

How do you handle my private data?

User privacy is a high priority for us. And because we're independent, we can actually operate that way. We collect only aggregate data, not personal data. For example, while we have no idea which users have favorited which books, we know how many users have favorited each book.
